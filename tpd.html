TASK 1: LO1 - INTERCULTURAL COMMUNICATION

Intercultural communication helps individuals in the professional sphere to understand
and respect individuals that have different cultural affiliations;
to respond appropriately, effectively and respectfully when interacting and communicating with such individuals;
to establish positive and constructive relationships.
Intercultural communication plays a huge role in effective communication,
and this is because we are all different,
it is important to recognise each other's communication manners,
and then learn to have awareness of other people’s culture, and ours.

In the Public Relations and Advertising field, there is always a huge emphasis on intercultural research.
This is because culture affects everything including advertisements, and when working with people across different backgrounds,
it is crucial to be aware of cultural differences. If a brand creates an advertising message for people, and it violates certain practices
in the culture, the brand suffers hostility. For instance, if a body wash brands disseminates an advertising message that shows a half naked woman,
no one will ever patronise them. Yes, it is patriarchy, but it is their culture,
and as outsiders we have zero or little impact on their culture.

A vivid intercultural experience I encountered in my course is the fact that most Chinese people generally like to distance themselves from people,
and stick with people from their country, and people who understand their language.
I did not realise this until I spoke Mandarin to them, and not long after then, they started including me in most of their conversations.
Another person would see this as a “snobbish” attitude, and would not understand that it is how they act.
Intercultural communication offers the ability to deal across cultures, which is increasingly important, as the world gets smaller.
Intercultural communication is so crucial because each employee’s unique background, life experiences and skillsets influence their work performance.
Inclusive companies have been proven to be nearly two times more more innovative than other companies thanks to the multiple perspectives of their employees.

TASK 1: LO3 - MANAGEMENT/LEADERSHIP

Project Management

Project Management is very important in all spheres, contextually, public relations.
In Public Relations, people/organisations undergo PR Campaigns. PR campaign is a series of planned activities that all have a specific purpose and are working towards the same goal for a business.
PR campaigns always have clear objectives and usually have a fixed time frame to them.
In order for a PR campaign to be successful, it has to undergo series of planning, and employ project management skills such as:
defining, planning, designing, implementing, testing and acceptance.

To develop a successful PR campaign, one must follow the following steps, because failing to plan means planning to fail 1. Many PR experts choose to use the SMART method as a guide

  • Specific: What is the desired outcome?
  • Measurable: How will success be measured?
  • Achievable: How will the campaign achieve its goals considering outside influences?
  • Realistic: Is your campaign goal realistic for the bandwidth of your campaign?
  • Time-based: How long do you have to meet these goals?

2. Select Your Target Audience.

3. Brainstorm

Consider these questions

  • What is the message?
  • Who is the message for?
  • What platforms will share the message?
  • What topic is the message and how will you get it across?

and how the hostility was turned into acceptance.
